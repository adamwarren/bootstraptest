Maris PiperNew!

Maris Piper is available in supermarkets and greengrocers, but not all year round. Maris Piper has a golden brown skin and creamy white flesh, with a floury texture. The tubers are oval, sometimes extended and shallow-eyed. Maris Piper has relatively high dry matter content, which is what gives them their fabulous floury texture. This makes them perfect for roast potatoes, which are best cooked by initially parboiling them to produce a textured surface, which in turn creates a wonderful crispy, crunchy outer roasted finish.

Kerr’s Pink

A favourite ‘shallow eyed’ potato variety, grown since the 1920’s in Ireland. Kerr’s Pink is a white flowered, late main crop potato which competes in the same category as the most popular Rooster variety. Kerr’s Pink has short oval tubers and are marketed as pre-packs and general ware.

Rooster

Rooster potatoes are easily distinguishable by their russet red skin and shallow eyes. Roosters have high dry matter content with a yellow flesh, which imparts a floury texture and deep earthy flavour. The Rooster variety lends itself particularly well to all types of potato preparation and cooking. Their ease of wash and peel is attractive to their speed of preparation.In terms of utility they are the ultimate all-rounder potato. Rooster is the most popular fresh ware potato variety in Ireland with consumers, with almost 6 in 10 potatoes grown and sold in Ireland being Rooster. Its quality, texture, utility and versatility, as well as its ease of use/preparation make it a very attractive potato choice for modern Irish consumers.
